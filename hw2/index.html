Section I: Bezier Curves and Surfaces

Part 1: Bezier curves with 1D de Casteljau subdivision

De Casteljau's algorithm is a recursive way to evaluate a bezier curve at a given parameter t. It interpolates between control points by lerping with parameter t and then recursively applies the same process to resulting points until a final point is reached. I implemented this algorithm by looping n - 1 times through a list of control points and lerping between them to get the next list of control points.

Part 2: Bezier surfaces with separable 1D de Casteljau

Bezier surfaces are a generalization of bezier curves to two dimensions. They are defined by a grid of control points and are evaluated by evaluating the bezier curve at each row and column of the grid.

Part 6: Loop subdivision for mesh upsampling

I implemented loop subdivision by following the instructions. I first computed the new positions for all vertices and edges. Then, I split every original edge. Then, I flipped any new edge that connected an old and new vertex. Then, I copied the new vertex positions into the final position.

Sharp corners and edges become smoother after subdivision. You can increase the smoothness by adding more splits.

Cube preprocessing to become more symmetrical:

I found that flipping opposite diagonals on the cube so that they are mirror symmetrical made the cube more symmetrical after subdivision. I think they occur because any assymetries in the mesh are amplified after subdivision, so we want to make sure to make the mesh as symmetrical as possible before subdivision.
